# Undercover Mob Boss — Brainstorm Session
*Captured March 15, 2026*

---

## The Big Idea

A digital-physical social deduction game. Everyone in the same room, phones in hand.
Same magic as Secret Hitler — the lying-to-your-face part — just modernized.

**We're on to something.**

---

## What Makes This Different

### 1. Original IP, Familiar Mechanics
- Secret Hitler is the inspiration, not the product
- Our skin: 1940s/50s noir city. Mob. Corruption. Citizens fighting back.
- No trademark issues. Fully ours.

### 2. The "Close Your Eyes" Problem — Solved
Wil Wheaton does the voiceover for Secret Hitler's eye-closing phase and it's legendary.
We're not losing that energy — we're *elevating* it.

Instead of theater and honor systems, your **phone handles the secret**:
- Game assigns roles privately
- Your phone buzzes and tells you exactly what you need to know
- Nobody else sees your screen
- Then the narrator speaks

> *"Citizens of Millbrook City... your fate has been sealed.*
> *Check your phone. Know your allegiance.*
> *And whatever you do... don't let it show."*

### 3. The Narrator Voice
- Full noir voiceover — gravelly, theatrical, a little menacing, fully committed to the bit
- Narrates every phase: role reveal, nominations, voting, policy flip, assassination, game over
- ElevenLabs TTS — nail the Wil Wheaton *energy* without the celeb
- This is what makes it legendary. People quote it for years.

Sample lines:
- *"The Mayor has nominated the Police Chief. The city watches. The mob waits."*
- *"The votes are in. Democracy... has spoken. Or has it?"*
- *"A policy has been enacted. The citizens cheer. The mob smiles."*
- *"The Mob Boss has been elected. Millbrook City belongs to the mob. Game over."*

### 4. Same Room, Modernized
- Physical presence is the point — don't Zoom this
- Phones = private information layer (role, team, knowledge)
- Shared screen (tablet or TV) = public game state, policy track, election results
- QR code / room code join — no app install, browser PWA

---

## The Experience Arc

1. Host creates game, shares QR code
2. Players scan → join on their phones
3. Narrator speaks as roles are distributed privately
4. Players check phones (their secret, no one else's)
5. Phones flip to game mode — shared screen shows the city
6. Game plays out with narrator punctuating every beat
7. Chaos, accusations, betrayal, laughter

---

## Why Build This

- **It's fun.** Actually fun. Not a demo, not a tech showcase.
- **Original IP** — could be a real product someday
- **Great MO v3 test bed** — real-time state machine, WebSockets, multiplayer sync, no ML
- **Technically interesting** — private view + shared view split, role secrecy, phase management
- **Party game distribution** — browser-based, no install, spreads by word of mouth
