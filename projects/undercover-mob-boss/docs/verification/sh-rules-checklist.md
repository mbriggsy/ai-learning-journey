# Secret Hitler — Atomic Rules Checklist

Extracted from the official Secret Hitler rulebook PDF. Every rule is one testable assertion.

---

## 1. ROLES — Distribution, Knowledge, Team Membership

```
[ROLES-01] The game supports 5 to 10 players.
[ROLES-02] Each player is secretly assigned exactly one role: Liberal, Fascist, or Hitler.
[ROLES-03] There is always exactly 1 Hitler in every game.
[ROLES-04] Hitler is a member of the Fascist team.
[ROLES-05] Liberals always have a majority of players.
[ROLES-06] In a 5-player game there are 3 Liberals, 1 Fascist, and 1 Hitler.
[ROLES-07] In a 6-player game there are 4 Liberals, 1 Fascist, and 1 Hitler.
[ROLES-08] In a 7-player game there are 4 Liberals, 2 Fascists, and 1 Hitler.
[ROLES-09] In an 8-player game there are 5 Liberals, 2 Fascists, and 1 Hitler.
[ROLES-10] In a 9-player game there are 5 Liberals, 3 Fascists, and 1 Hitler.
[ROLES-11] In a 10-player game there are 6 Liberals, 3 Fascists, and 1 Hitler.
[ROLES-12] Each player has both a Secret Role card and a Party Membership card.
[ROLES-13] Liberal Secret Role cards are always paired with a Liberal Party Membership card.
[ROLES-14] Fascist Secret Role cards are always paired with a Fascist Party Membership card.
[ROLES-15] Hitler's Secret Role card is always paired with a Fascist Party Membership card.
[ROLES-16] Hitler's Party Membership card shows Fascist loyalty but does not reveal the Hitler role.
[ROLES-17] Roles are assigned randomly (envelopes shuffled before distribution).
[ROLES-18] Players examine their Secret Role cards in secret (not shown to others).
```

### Night Phase — 5-6 Players

```
[ROLES-19] In a 5-6 player game, Fascist(s) and Hitler open their eyes and acknowledge each other.
[ROLES-20] In a 5-6 player game, both Hitler and Fascists know each other's identities.
```

### Night Phase — 7-10 Players

```
[ROLES-21] In a 7-10 player game, Fascists (not Hitler) open their eyes and acknowledge each other.
[ROLES-22] In a 7-10 player game, Hitler keeps eyes closed but extends a thumbs-up gesture.
[ROLES-23] In a 7-10 player game, Fascists observe the thumbs-up to identify Hitler.
[ROLES-24] In a 7-10 player game, Fascists know who Hitler is.
[ROLES-25] In a 7-10 player game, Fascists know who the other Fascists are.
[ROLES-26] In a 7-10 player game, Hitler does NOT know who the Fascists are.
[ROLES-27] Liberals never learn any identity information during the night phase.
```

## 2. DECK — Composition, Shuffle, Reshuffle Triggers

```
[DECK-01] The Policy deck contains exactly 17 tiles total.
[DECK-02] The Policy deck contains exactly 6 Liberal Policy tiles.
[DECK-03] The Policy deck contains exactly 11 Fascist Policy tiles.
[DECK-04] At setup, all 17 Policy tiles are shuffled into a single face-down deck.
[DECK-05] The deck is placed face down on the Draw pile card at setup.
[DECK-06] If fewer than 3 tiles remain in the Policy deck at the END of a Legislative Session, shuffle the draw pile and discard pile together to form a new Policy deck.
[DECK-07] If fewer than 3 tiles remain in the Policy deck when the Election Tracker triggers a chaos enactment, shuffle them with the Discard pile to create a new Policy deck.
[DECK-08] When reshuffling, unused Policy tiles should never be revealed to players.
[DECK-09] When reshuffling, remaining tiles should NOT be simply placed on top of the new deck — they must be shuffled in.
[DECK-10] Discarded Policy tiles are placed face down into the Discard pile.
[DECK-11] Discarded Policy tiles should never be revealed to the group.
```

## 3. ELECTION — Rotation, Nomination, Eligibility, Voting, Majority

### Presidential Candidacy Rotation

```
[ELEC-01] The first Presidential Candidate is selected randomly.
[ELEC-02] The first Presidential Candidate receives both the President and Chancellor placards.
[ELEC-03] At the beginning of each new round, the President placard moves clockwise to the next player.
[ELEC-04] The player who receives the President placard becomes the new Presidential Candidate.
[ELEC-05] Dead/executed players are skipped in the presidential rotation.
```

### Nomination

```
[ELEC-06] The Presidential Candidate nominates one Chancellor Candidate.
[ELEC-07] The Presidential Candidate may nominate any other eligible player as Chancellor Candidate.
[ELEC-08] The Presidential Candidate cannot nominate themselves as Chancellor Candidate.
[ELEC-09] The Presidential Candidate is free to discuss Chancellor options with the table before nominating.
```

### Voting

```
[ELEC-10] Every player (including both Candidates) votes on the proposed government.
[ELEC-11] Each player votes either Ja! (yes) or Nein (no).
[ELEC-12] Players may discuss the proposed government before voting.
[ELEC-13] All votes are revealed simultaneously (not one by one).
[ELEC-14] All votes are public once revealed.
[ELEC-15] If a majority of players votes yes, the election succeeds.
[ELEC-16] If a majority of players votes no, the election fails.
[ELEC-17] If the vote is tied, the election fails.
```

### Election Success

```
[ELEC-18] On a successful election, the Presidential Candidate becomes President.
[ELEC-19] On a successful election, the Chancellor Candidate becomes Chancellor.
[ELEC-20] If 3 or more Fascist Policies have been enacted, the newly elected Chancellor must reveal whether they are Hitler.
[ELEC-21] If the newly elected Chancellor IS Hitler and 3+ Fascist Policies are enacted, the Fascists win immediately.
[ELEC-22] If the newly elected Chancellor is NOT Hitler and 3+ Fascist Policies are enacted, all players now know for certain that Chancellor is not Hitler.
[ELEC-23] After a successful election (and Hitler check if applicable), the game proceeds to the Legislative Session.
```

### Election Failure

```
[ELEC-24] On a failed election, the Presidential Candidate does not become President.
[ELEC-25] On a failed election, the President placard moves clockwise to the next player.
[ELEC-26] On a failed election, the Election Tracker advances by one.
```

## 4. ELECTION TRACKER — Advancement, Auto-Enact, Side Effects

```
[TRACK-01] The Election Tracker starts at zero.
[TRACK-02] The Election Tracker advances by one each time an election fails.
[TRACK-03] If the Election Tracker reaches 3 (three consecutive failed elections), a chaos enactment occurs.
[TRACK-04] During a chaos enactment, the top Policy tile of the draw pile is immediately revealed and enacted.
[TRACK-05] A Policy enacted via the Election Tracker is placed on the corresponding track (Liberal or Fascist) just like any other enacted Policy.
[TRACK-06] Any Presidential Power granted by a Policy enacted via the Election Tracker is IGNORED (not used).
[TRACK-07] After a chaos enactment, the Election Tracker resets to zero.
[TRACK-08] After a chaos enactment, all existing term limits are cleared/forgotten.
[TRACK-09] After a chaos enactment, all players become eligible to hold the office of Chancellor for the next Election.
[TRACK-10] If fewer than 3 tiles remain in the Policy deck at the time of a chaos enactment, shuffle the draw and discard piles together to create a new deck.
[TRACK-11] Any time a new Policy tile is placed face-up on a track, the Election Tracker resets to zero — whether enacted by a government or by the tracker itself.
[TRACK-12] The Election Tracker resets when a government successfully enacts a Policy.
[TRACK-13] The Election Tracker resets when the tracker itself auto-enacts a Policy.
```

## 5. LEGISLATIVE SESSION — Draw/Discard Flow, Communication Rules, Secrecy

### Draw and Discard Flow

```
[LEGIS-01] The President draws the top 3 tiles from the Policy deck.
[LEGIS-02] The President looks at the 3 drawn tiles in secret.
[LEGIS-03] The President discards exactly 1 of the 3 tiles face down into the Discard pile.
[LEGIS-04] The President passes the remaining 2 tiles to the Chancellor.
[LEGIS-05] The President must hand both tiles to the Chancellor at the same time (not one at a time).
[LEGIS-06] The Chancellor looks at the 2 received tiles in secret.
[LEGIS-07] The Chancellor discards exactly 1 of the 2 tiles face down into the Discard pile.
[LEGIS-08] The Chancellor enacts the remaining 1 tile by placing it face up on the corresponding policy track.
[LEGIS-09] A Liberal Policy tile is placed on the Liberal track.
[LEGIS-10] A Fascist Policy tile is placed on the Fascist track.
```

### Communication Restrictions

```
[LEGIS-11] Verbal communication between the President and Chancellor is forbidden during the Legislative Session.
[LEGIS-12] Nonverbal communication between the President and Chancellor is forbidden during the Legislative Session.
[LEGIS-13] The President and Chancellor MAY NOT pick policies at random.
[LEGIS-14] The President and Chancellor MAY NOT shuffle tiles before discarding.
[LEGIS-15] The President and Chancellor MAY NOT use any other clever procedure to avoid secretly and intentionally selecting a policy.
[LEGIS-16] Attempting to telegraph hand contents via randomness or unusual selection procedure is a rules violation.
```

### Secrecy

```
[LEGIS-17] Discarded Policy tiles should never be revealed to the group.
[LEGIS-18] Players must rely on the word of the President and Chancellor regarding what policies were drawn.
[LEGIS-19] The President and Chancellor are free to lie about what policies they saw.
```

### Post-Legislative Session

```
[LEGIS-20] If fewer than 3 tiles remain in the Policy deck at the end of a Legislative Session, reshuffle (draw + discard piles combined).
[LEGIS-21] When reshuffling after a Legislative Session, unused tiles must not be revealed.
[LEGIS-22] When reshuffling after a Legislative Session, remaining tiles must not simply be placed on top — they must be shuffled in.
[LEGIS-23] If a Fascist Policy was enacted that covers a Presidential Power slot, proceed to Executive Action.
[LEGIS-24] If a Liberal Policy was enacted, begin a new round with a new Election (no Executive Action).
[LEGIS-25] If a Fascist Policy was enacted but it grants no Presidential Power, begin a new round with a new Election.
```

## 6. EXECUTIVE POWERS — Who Uses Them, Rules, Power Grid

### General Executive Power Rules

```
[EXEC-01] Presidential Powers are triggered only when a Fascist Policy is enacted by an elected government that covers a power slot on the Fascist track.
[EXEC-02] The sitting President (the one who was part of the legislative session) uses the Presidential Power.
[EXEC-03] It does not matter what team the President is on — even Liberals use the power.
[EXEC-04] The President MUST use the power before the next round can begin.
[EXEC-05] Gameplay cannot continue until the President uses the power.
[EXEC-06] Presidential Powers are single-use — they don't stack or roll over to future turns.
[EXEC-07] Before using a power, the President is free to discuss the issue with other players.
[EXEC-08] Ultimately, the President alone decides how and when the power is used.
[EXEC-09] Presidential Powers are NOT triggered by policies enacted via the Election Tracker (chaos enactment).
```

### Power Grid — 5-6 Players

```
[GRID-01] In a 5-6 player game, the 1st Fascist Policy grants NO power.
[GRID-02] In a 5-6 player game, the 2nd Fascist Policy grants NO power.
[GRID-03] In a 5-6 player game, the 3rd Fascist Policy grants Policy Peek.
[GRID-04] In a 5-6 player game, the 4th Fascist Policy grants Execution.
[GRID-05] In a 5-6 player game, the 5th Fascist Policy grants Execution.
```

### Power Grid — 7-8 Players

```
[GRID-06] In a 7-8 player game, the 1st Fascist Policy grants NO power.
[GRID-07] In a 7-8 player game, the 2nd Fascist Policy grants Investigate Loyalty.
[GRID-08] In a 7-8 player game, the 3rd Fascist Policy grants Call Special Election.
[GRID-09] In a 7-8 player game, the 4th Fascist Policy grants Execution.
[GRID-10] In a 7-8 player game, the 5th Fascist Policy grants Execution.
```

### Power Grid — 9-10 Players

```
[GRID-11] In a 9-10 player game, the 1st Fascist Policy grants Investigate Loyalty.
[GRID-12] In a 9-10 player game, the 2nd Fascist Policy grants Investigate Loyalty.
[GRID-13] In a 9-10 player game, the 3rd Fascist Policy grants Call Special Election.
[GRID-14] In a 9-10 player game, the 4th Fascist Policy grants Execution.
[GRID-15] In a 9-10 player game, the 5th Fascist Policy grants Execution.
```

### Investigate Loyalty

```
[INVEST-01] The President chooses one player to investigate.
[INVEST-02] The investigated player hands their Party Membership card (NOT their Secret Role card) to the President.
[INVEST-03] The President checks the investigated player's party loyalty in secret.
[INVEST-04] The President returns the Party Membership card to the investigated player after viewing it.
[INVEST-05] The investigation reveals only party membership (Liberal or Fascist), not the specific role.
[INVEST-06] Hitler's Party Membership card shows Fascist — investigation cannot distinguish Hitler from an ordinary Fascist.
[INVEST-07] The President may share the results of the investigation at their discretion.
[INVEST-08] The President may lie about the results of the investigation.
[INVEST-09] No player may be investigated twice in the same game.
```

### Call Special Election

```
[SPECIAL-01] The President chooses any other player at the table to be the next Presidential Candidate.
[SPECIAL-02] The chosen player receives the President placard.
[SPECIAL-03] Any player can be chosen for a Special Election, including term-limited players.
[SPECIAL-04] The Special Election President nominates an eligible Chancellor Candidate and the election proceeds as usual.
[SPECIAL-05] A Special Election does NOT skip any players in the normal rotation.
[SPECIAL-06] After a Special Election, the President placard returns to the left of the President who ENACTED the Special Election (i.e., the player who used the power).
[SPECIAL-07] If the President passes the presidency to the next player in the normal rotation, that player gets to run for President twice in a row (once for the Special Election, once for their normal turn).
```

### Policy Peek

```
[PEEK-01] The President secretly looks at the top 3 tiles in the Policy deck.
[PEEK-02] The President returns the tiles to the top of the deck without changing the order.
[PEEK-03] The President may share what they saw (or lie about it).
```

### Execution

```
[EXECUTE-01] The President executes one player at the table.
[EXECUTE-02] The President announces the execution formally (e.g., "I formally execute [player name]").
[EXECUTE-03] If the executed player is Hitler, the game ends immediately in a Liberal victory.
[EXECUTE-04] If the executed player is NOT Hitler, the player's role (Liberal or Fascist) is NOT revealed to the table.
[EXECUTE-05] Players must work out for themselves whether a Liberal or Fascist was killed.
[EXECUTE-06] Executed players are removed from the game.
[EXECUTE-07] Executed players may not speak.
[EXECUTE-08] Executed players may not vote.
[EXECUTE-09] Executed players may not run for office (President or Chancellor).
```

## 7. VETO — When Available, Flow, Tracker Interaction

```
[VETO-01] The Veto Power becomes available after the 5th Fascist Policy has been enacted.
[VETO-02] The Veto Power is permanent once activated — it applies to ALL subsequent Legislative Sessions.
[VETO-03] During a veto-eligible Legislative Session, the President draws 3 tiles, discards 1, and passes 2 to the Chancellor as normal.
[VETO-04] Instead of enacting either Policy, the Chancellor may say "I wish to veto this agenda."
[VETO-05] The veto requires the President's consent — the President may say "I agree to the veto."
[VETO-06] If the President consents to the veto, both remaining Policy tiles are discarded.
[VETO-07] If the President consents to the veto, no Policy is enacted.
[VETO-08] If the President consents to the veto, the President placard passes to the left as usual (new round begins).
[VETO-09] If the President does NOT consent to the veto, the Chancellor MUST enact one of the two Policy tiles as normal.
[VETO-10] Each use of the Veto Power (successful veto) advances the Election Tracker by one.
[VETO-11] A successful veto counts as an inactive government for Election Tracker purposes.
```

## 8. WIN CONDITIONS — All Paths to Victory

### Liberal Win Conditions

```
[WIN-01] Liberals win if 5 Liberal Policies are enacted on the Liberal track.
[WIN-02] Liberals win if Hitler is assassinated/executed.
[WIN-03] The Liberal victory by execution is immediate — the game ends as soon as Hitler is executed.
```

### Fascist Win Conditions

```
[WIN-04] Fascists win if 6 Fascist Policies are enacted on the Fascist track.
[WIN-05] Fascists win if Hitler is elected Chancellor after 3 or more Fascist Policies have been enacted.
[WIN-06] The Fascist victory by Hitler election is immediate — the game ends as soon as a successful election installs Hitler as Chancellor with 3+ Fascist Policies already enacted.
[WIN-07] The Hitler election win condition does NOT apply if fewer than 3 Fascist Policies have been enacted.
[WIN-08] The Hitler election check occurs on every successful Chancellor election once 3+ Fascist Policies are enacted.
```

## 9. TERM LIMITS — Eligibility Restrictions

```
[TERM-01] The last elected President is term-limited.
[TERM-02] The last elected Chancellor is term-limited.
[TERM-03] Term-limited players are ineligible to be nominated as Chancellor Candidate.
[TERM-04] Term limits apply to the last ELECTED President and Chancellor — not the last nominated pair.
[TERM-05] A failed nomination/election does NOT change who is term-limited.
[TERM-06] Term limits only affect nominations to the Chancellorship — they do NOT restrict who can be President.
[TERM-07] Any player can be President, even someone who was just Chancellor.
[TERM-08] If there are only 5 players left in the game, only the last elected Chancellor is term-limited (not the last President).
[TERM-09] In a 5-player game (or when 5 players remain), the last elected President MAY be nominated as Chancellor Candidate.
[TERM-10] Term limits are cleared/forgotten after a chaos enactment (Election Tracker reaches 3).
[TERM-11] After term limits are cleared by chaos enactment, all players become eligible to hold the office of Chancellor for the next Election.
```

## 10. INFORMATION RULES — Revealed, Hidden, Lying

### What Is Revealed

```
[INFO-01] All votes are revealed simultaneously and are public.
[INFO-02] Enacted Policy tiles are placed face-up and visible to all players.
[INFO-03] The number of enacted Liberal Policies is public knowledge (visible on the track).
[INFO-04] The number of enacted Fascist Policies is public knowledge (visible on the track).
[INFO-05] The Election Tracker position is public knowledge.
[INFO-06] When a Chancellor is elected with 3+ Fascist Policies enacted and is NOT Hitler, this fact (that they are not Hitler) becomes public knowledge.
[INFO-07] If an executed player is Hitler, this is revealed (game ends).
```

### What Stays Hidden

```
[INFO-08] Players' Secret Role cards remain hidden throughout the game (except in game-ending scenarios).
[INFO-09] Players' Party Membership cards remain hidden (except when shown via Investigation).
[INFO-10] Discarded Policy tiles are never revealed to the group.
[INFO-11] The contents of the Policy deck are hidden.
[INFO-12] If an executed player is NOT Hitler, their role (Liberal or Fascist) is NOT revealed.
[INFO-13] The Policy tiles the President drew during a Legislative Session are hidden from all other players.
[INFO-14] The Policy tile the President discarded during a Legislative Session is hidden.
[INFO-15] The Policy tile the Chancellor discarded during a Legislative Session is hidden.
[INFO-16] Policy Peek results are known only to the President (hidden from others).
[INFO-17] Investigation results are known only to the President (hidden from others).
```

### Lying Rules

```
[INFO-18] Players may always lie about hidden knowledge in Secret Hitler.
[INFO-19] The President may lie about what Policy tiles were drawn.
[INFO-20] The Chancellor may lie about what Policy tiles were received.
[INFO-21] The President may lie about Investigation results.
[INFO-22] The President may lie about Policy Peek results.
[INFO-23] A player who is Hitler MUST truthfully reveal their role if assassinated/executed.
[INFO-24] A player who is Hitler MUST truthfully reveal their role if elected Chancellor after 3+ Fascist Policies have been enacted.
[INFO-25] The ONLY times players MUST tell the truth are in game-ending, Hitler-related scenarios (execution of Hitler, election of Hitler as Chancellor with 3+ Fascist Policies).
```

## 11. GAME STRUCTURE — Round Flow and Sequencing

```
[FLOW-01] The game is played in rounds.
[FLOW-02] Each round has up to three phases: Election, Legislative Session, and Executive Action.
[FLOW-03] A round always begins with an Election.
[FLOW-04] A Legislative Session occurs only after a successful election.
[FLOW-05] An Executive Action occurs only if the Legislative Session enacts a Fascist Policy that grants a Presidential Power.
[FLOW-06] After a failed election, a new round begins with the next Presidential Candidate (no Legislative Session).
[FLOW-07] After a Liberal Policy is enacted, a new round begins with a new Election.
[FLOW-08] After a Fascist Policy with no power is enacted, a new round begins with a new Election.
[FLOW-09] After a Fascist Policy with a power is enacted, the Executive Action must complete before a new round begins.
```

## 12. SETUP — Initial Game State

```
[SETUP-01] The Fascist track board is selected based on the number of players (5-6, 7-8, or 9-10).
[SETUP-02] The Fascist track is placed next to any Liberal track (one Liberal track for all player counts).
[SETUP-03] All 17 Policy tiles are shuffled into a single deck at setup.
[SETUP-04] The shuffled deck is placed face down on the Draw pile card.
[SETUP-05] The Discard pile is empty at the start of the game.
[SETUP-06] The Election Tracker starts at zero.
[SETUP-07] No policies are enacted at the start of the game (both tracks empty).
[SETUP-08] The first Presidential Candidate is selected randomly.
[SETUP-09] The first Presidential Candidate receives both the President and Chancellor placards.
```
