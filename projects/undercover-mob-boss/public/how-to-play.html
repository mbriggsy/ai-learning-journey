How to Play

A social deduction game for 5–10 players.
Same room. Phones out.

Scroll

"Millbrook City is a powder keg. The mob has planted operatives inside city hall. They sit at the same table as honest citizens. They vote on policy. They run for office. Nobody knows who's who. Somewhere among them is the Mob Boss — and the city is running out of time."

The 30-Second Briefing
01.

Two teams. Citizens vs. the Mob. Roles are secret. Citizens don't know anyone's identity.

02.

Each round: A Mayor nominates a Police Chief. Everyone votes. If it passes, the pair secretly enacts policy.

03.

The deck is rigged. 11 bad cards, 6 good. Bad policy will happen. The question is who let it happen.

04.

Citizens win with 5 Good Policies or by executing the Mob Boss.

05.

Mob wins with 6 Bad Policies or sneaking the Boss into the Police Chief seat.

06.

Lying is the game. Accuse. Defend. Bluff. Trust no one in Millbrook.

II — Operations Brief

Setup

  1. One person hosts on a tablet or laptop — the shared screen everyone sees.
  2. Players join on their phones — scan the QR code or type the URL with the 4-letter room code.
  3. Once 5–10 players have joined, the host taps Start Game.
  4. Roles are secretly assigned. The first Mayor is chosen at random.
  5. Check your phone privately. Tap your card to reveal your role. Tap again to seal it.
  6. The game begins when everyone has sealed their cards.

IV — The Cycle of Power

A Round of Play

1. Nomination

The Mayor nominates a Police Chief from the other players. The Mayor cannot nominate themselves or anyone who is term-limited (served in the last elected government).

2. Election

Everyone votes on their phone simultaneously — Approve or Deny. More than half must approve. Ties fail.

Approved → The government is formed. Move to Policy Session.
Denied → Election Tracker advances. Next player becomes Mayor. Three consecutive failures force the top card to be auto-enacted — no vote, no choice.

3. Policy Session

The Mayor and Police Chief work in secret on their phones. No talking.

Mayor draws 3 → discards 1 → passes 2 to Chief.
Chief discards 1 → enacts the remaining card.

Both may lie about what they saw. This is where the deduction begins.

4. Executive Power

When certain Bad Policies are enacted, the Mayor gains a one-time power — used immediately.

Three Failed Elections

If three elections fail in a row, the top policy is enacted automatically. No executive power triggers. The tracker resets and all term limits are cleared.

Veto Power

After 5 Bad Policies, the Chief may propose to veto — discarding both cards. The Mayor decides. If accepted, no policy is enacted but the tracker advances.

Policy Peek

Secretly view the top 3 cards. May share — or lie.

Investigation

Learn if a player is Mob or Citizen. Boss shows as "Mob." No player investigated twice.

Special Nomination

Pick the next Mayor. Even term-limited players are eligible.

Execution

Eliminate a player. Their role stays hidden. If it's the Boss — Citizens win instantly.

V — Endgame

How Millbrook Falls — or Survives

Citizens Victory

  • 5 Good Policies are enacted

— or —

  • The Mob Boss is executed

The Mob Wins

  • 6 Bad Policies are enacted

— or —

  • The Mob Boss becomes Police Chief after 3+ bad policies
The Mob Boss Election Rule: Once 3+ Bad Policies are on the board, if the Mob Boss is elected Police Chief — the mob wins instantly. But if someone is elected and the game continues, everyone knows for certain that player is NOT the Boss. Use that.
VI — Case File

A Night in Millbrook

Dossier Entry: Round 3

Sal (Mayor) draws three cards on his phone: 2 Bad, 1 Good. He discards a Bad card and passes 1 Bad + 1 Good to Vince (Police Chief).

Vince looks at the two cards. He discards the Bad one and enacts the Good Policy. Millbrook breathes.

But then Sal tells the table: "I drew three bad cards. Vince got two bad cards. I don't know how a good policy came out."

Vince fires back: "He's lying — he gave me one of each and I played the good one."

Someone is lying. The table erupts. That's the game.

VII — Rules of Engagement

Table Talk

Everything is allowed. Lie, bluff, accuse, make deals, promise things you won't keep.

The only restrictions:

  • You cannot show your phone screen to another player
  • Once a vote begins, discussion stops until results are revealed
VIII — Expert Intelligence

Strategy Tips

For Citizens

  • Watch voting patterns. Consistent approval of suspicious candidates is a tell.
  • Pay attention to who defends whom after a bad policy.
  • When the Chief blames the Mayor — one of them is probably lying.

For Soldiers

  • Sometimes enacting good policy builds trust. Play the long game.
  • Protect the Boss at all costs. If suspicion lands on them, redirect it.

For the Boss

  • Play like a Citizen. Vote like a Citizen. Talk like a Citizen.
  • Let your soldiers do the dirty work.
  • The Police Chief seat is your throne — wait for the right moment.
